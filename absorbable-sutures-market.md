<p><p>The absorbable sutures market includes various types based on their material composition. Catgut sutures, derived from natural sheep or cow intestines, are widely used due to their biodegradability and ease of absorption. Polymer chemistry sutures, such as polylactic acid and polyglycolic acid, offer controlled absorption rates and enhanced tensile strength. Collagen sutures, made from animal-derived collagen, provide a natural alternative with good biocompatibility. Other categories may include synthetic variants and innovative bioengineered materials that meet specific surgical needs.</p></p>
