<p><p>The screws market comprises various types, with self-tapping and non-self-tapping screws being prominent categories. Self-tapping screws are designed to cut their own threads into materials such as wood or metal, allowing for easy installation without pre-drilling. In contrast, non-self-tapping screws require a pre-drilled hole for insertion, providing a secure fastening where precise alignment is crucial. The demand for these screws varies across industries like furniture, construction, and automotive, driven by factors such as material compatibility and application requirements.</p></p>
